Ole Lehmann argues that pure education creators face an existential threat in 2026: the traditional "how to do X" content format loses its value proposition when any viewer can feed the video to an LLM and get the same answer in 10 seconds. This structural shift decouples educational value from creator revenue, forcing creators to pivot toward personality-driven content, community, or entertainment rather than information delivery.

## Key Takeaways

- Pure "how to do X" educational content is being commoditized by LLMs, collapsing the value-to-view ratio for educational creators
- Creators who survive will differentiate on personality, entertainment, and community — not information density
- The shift signals a broader restructuring of creator economics as AI erodes the scarcity of packaged knowledge
