A practitioner's guide to "AI discovery" — the emerging layer of SEO that determines whether LLMs like ChatGPT recommend your brand when users ask product/service questions. The argument is that traditional SEO optimizes for human click-through on search results, but 40%+ of AI users now get recommendations directly from LLMs, bypassing search entirely. Companies that rank well in LLM answers win the sale before competitors even get a click.

The framework has three pillars: (1) Solution Validation — building pages that explicitly validate your product for specific buyer scenarios and comparisons rather than generic category pages, because LLMs rank based on how clearly you demonstrate solving a specific problem for a specific buyer type; (2) Programmatic Coverage — systematically creating structured pages for every comparison, buyer question variation, and decision stage, since LLM confidence in recommending a brand correlates with how many times it sees that brand associated with the same problem across different sources; (3) Entity Velocity — building fresh, active mentions across communities, reviews, comparisons, and industry content, because LLMs penalize brands that haven't been recently discussed.

Case studies: a B2B EdTech client rebuilt their site around solution validation and appeared in 30+ AI-generated recommendations within a month, while organic traffic grew from 6,000 to 60,000+ monthly visits. A B2B SaaS client went from under 10 to 250+ daily non-branded clicks (7,500/mo) in a timeframe that would've taken years through traditional link-building.

## Key Takeaways

- Over 40% of AI users prefer AI search over Google; ChatGPT has billions of monthly visits — this is a real acquisition channel
- LLMs pull from a small set of trusted sources; if you're not in that source set, you don't appear in answers
- Solution Validation: build pages that explicitly answer "what's the best X for Y?" and "A vs. B?" rather than generic category copy
- Programmatic Coverage: appear for 50 variations of the same buyer problem so LLMs treat the association as fact
- Entity Velocity: fresh, active mentions across communities and review platforms signal currency to LLMs
- The new buyer journey is "ask AI → get shortlist → maybe search" — you need to win at step 1
- Companies with solid existing SEO fundamentals are 80% of the way there; the remaining 20% is restructuring for AI readability
