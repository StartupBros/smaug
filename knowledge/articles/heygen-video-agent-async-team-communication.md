Nick Warner's growth team at HeyGen has been using their own Video Agent product internally for 6+ months before public launch. Every Friday, the 38-person cross-functional team (engineering, paid media, product, content, AI workflows, social) submits short agent-produced videos recapping what they worked on, what shipped, where they saw traction, blockers, and what needs review. The agent scripts, styles, adds captions, motion, and pacing automatically—so team members can communicate with the clarity of a creative director at the speed of a Slack message.

The key outcome is what Warner calls "emotional alignment": async communication that lets you hear tone, feel momentum, and share in the story of what the team is building—not just tickets and dashboards. This replaced ad-hoc 30-minute sync calls with a structured weekly ritual that creates collective momentum across the org. The team also uses Video Agent for narrating PRDs, visualizing complex engineering systems for non-technical stakeholders, and async onboarding documentation.

## Key Takeaways

- Agent-scripted weekly video updates replaced sync standups for a 38-person growth org
- Emotional alignment is a distinct outcome from information transfer—hearing tone and momentum builds shared context that text can't replicate
- HeyGen dogfooded Video Agent V2 for 6 months before any external access, validating it as a real internal operating system
- AI as modality shift: teams won't just use AI tools—they'll communicate in higher resolution because of them
- The workflow also applies to PRD narration, engineering diagrams for non-technical audiences, and onboarding content
