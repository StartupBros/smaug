Antoine Rousseaux argues that the SaaS subscription model is collapsing under the weight of subscription fatigue — businesses bleeding thousands per month on tools they half-use, with dark-pattern cancellation flows designed to trap them. The real shift isn't back to "buy once, own forever" but to "subscribe to the builder, build everything else yourself." With Claude Max at $200/month, he cancelled $2,000+/month in SaaS and built custom replacements himself — a video editor that removes silences, improves audio, and outputs for YouTube — despite not knowing Python or JavaScript.

The article cites Linus Torvalds admitting AI writes better code than he can by hand, Anthropic building an entire product in 10 days with AI-written code, and solo developers like Paulius competing directly with VC-funded startups using Claude Code alone. Rousseaux predicts one-person companies will explode, lifetime deals will outsell subscriptions, and staff costs for small businesses will collapse. The winners: AI infrastructure providers (Claude, Cursor, compute), lifetime-deal tools, hyper-niche single-purpose apps, and communities teaching people to build their own software.

## Key Takeaways

- The subscription model became exploitative — easy to join, painful to cancel, $49-199/month per tool for features most users never touch
- AI (especially Claude Code) lets non-developers build custom tools that do exactly what they need, eliminating the need for most SaaS subscriptions
- Linus Torvalds now uses AI to write code; Anthropic shipped a product in 10 days entirely with AI-written code
- Solo developers are competing with and beating VC-funded companies using Claude Code
- The new model: 1-3 AI subscriptions that enable building everything else, replacing 100+ scattered SaaS tools
- Lifetime deals will surge in conversion as people seek ownership over endless rental
