An argument that we are moving toward an "orchestration economy" where AI agents handle the execution layer of business operations, leaving humans to function as orchestrators of departments rather than knowledge workers completing individual tasks. The author runs nearly 1,000 skills and hundreds of agents via Claude Code for non-coding business functions: file conversion, email and meeting prep, hiring, compliance, repricing, sales activities, and procurement — at a scale of $778 in tokens and 334 million tokens per day.

The deeper claim is that once every routine business function can be codified into a skill or script, the nature of work shifts from doing to directing. Everyone in a company can "work closer to the speed of thought." The article's title frames this as a civilizational question: what changes structurally when everything that can be systematized, is?

## Key Takeaways

- Claude Code and custom agents are being used for pure business workflow automation, not just code generation
- The economics are already viable at scale: hundreds of sessions per day running business-critical tasks
- The transition from knowledge worker to orchestrator is the key career/org shift to prepare for
