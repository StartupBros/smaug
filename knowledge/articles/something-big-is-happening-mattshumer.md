Matt Shumer, a six-year AI startup founder and investor, wrote this essay as a direct warning to non-technical people in his life about the scale and speed of AI disruption already underway. He uses the COVID analogy: we're in the "this seems overblown" phase of something far larger. The essay documents how, as of early February 2026, new model releases (GPT-5.3 Codex from OpenAI and Opus 4.6 from Anthropic) have pushed AI past the threshold where it executes complete multi-day engineering projects — autonomously: writing, testing, iterating, and self-reviewing — without human intervention. Shumer describes walking away from his computer for four hours and returning to find finished, production-quality software with no corrections needed.

The essay covers METR's task-duration benchmarks (AI can now complete five-hour expert tasks end-to-end, doubling roughly every 4–7 months), the recursive AI self-improvement loop (GPT-5.3 Codex was instrumental in its own creation), Dario Amodei's prediction of 50% of entry-level white-collar job displacement within 1–5 years, and the intelligence explosion scenario of AI smarter than most PhDs arriving by 2026–2027. Shumer argues no knowledge-work field is immune — law, finance, medicine, writing, customer service — and that the "free tier" gap in public perception is masking just how capable current paid models already are.

The final sections shift from threat to opportunity: barriers to building (coding, writing, learning) are now essentially gone for anyone willing to pay $20/month for a capable model, and the most durable advantage is building the habit of adapting to a constantly changing toolset rather than mastering any single one.

## Key Takeaways

- As of Feb 5, 2026: AI can write tens of thousands of lines of code, self-test, iterate, and deliver finished software with no human review needed
- METR data: AI task autonomy duration has been doubling every 4–7 months; latest models handle ~5-hour expert tasks end-to-end
- GPT-5.3 Codex was used in its own creation — recursive self-improvement loop is confirmed and accelerating
- Amodei's prediction: 50% of entry-level white-collar jobs displaced within 1–5 years; many in the industry think this is conservative
- Public perception lags reality by ~1–2 years due to free-tier access; paying users on best models see something categorically different
- The new opportunity: knowledge, tutoring, and software-building costs are near zero — barriers to realizing personal projects have largely collapsed
- Most durable skill: adapting rapidly to new tools, not mastering any one of them
