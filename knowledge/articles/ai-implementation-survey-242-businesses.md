Luke Pierce ran four lead magnets on X and LinkedIn over several months, collecting detailed survey responses from 242 business owners about their AI challenges, goals, and budgets. The dataset covers companies ranging from 2-person shops to enterprises with $100M+ revenue, skewing heavily toward professional services (31%), SaaS/tech (18%), and real estate (14%). 37% report $500K+ annual revenue, with 21% doing $5M+.

The single biggest finding: 52% of respondents said "I don't know where to start or what to prioritize" — not budget constraints, not team resistance. Only 4% cited budget as a blocker. 61% have already tried AI, with 25% having tried it and gotten limited results (the most actionable segment: they already believe but failed at execution). The education barrier is essentially gone; the execution gap is where the opportunity lives.

Budget segmentation reveals two completely different customer profiles. Companies at $1K–$5K budgets are overwhelmed and need a guide (57% don't know where to start). Companies at $15K+ are past that stage — their blockers are team buy-in (22%), ROI justification to stakeholders (16%), and internal alignment. Same product, entirely different sales motion.

On desired outcomes: 44% want internal process automation and time savings; only 18% named revenue growth as a primary goal. "We'll save you X hours/week" outperforms "we'll increase revenue" consistently. Professional services is the highest-yield vertical because they sell time and AI saves time — the ROI math is obvious. Manufacturing, at only 6% of leads, punches above its weight in deal size and is largely underpenetrated by AI agencies.

The organic pipeline implied by the data: 193 leads, estimated $1.2M–$2.8M+ value. Zero ad spend. Five discovery calls directly from the typeform, all at $15K+ budgets, combined pipeline $75K–$250K+.

## Key Takeaways

- 52% of business owners need a clear roadmap, not a technology pitch — they're direction shopping, not price shopping
- Budget is a non-issue for most: only 4% cited it as a blocker, and 54 companies in the dataset had $5K+ budgets
- The 25% who tried AI and got limited results are the hottest segment — already bought in, just need execution
- Small-budget vs large-budget buyers require fundamentally different sales conversations; selling the same way to both loses deals
- Lead with time saved, not revenue generated — efficiency dominates desired outcomes 2.4:1 over growth
- Professional services is the easiest vertical; manufacturing is underpenetrated with high-revenue potential
- Content-driven lead magnets pre-qualify by demonstrating expertise before prospects ever get on a call
