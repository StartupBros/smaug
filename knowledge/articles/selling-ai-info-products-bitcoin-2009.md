A long-form sales pitch disguised as market analysis, arguing that the window for monetizing AI skills via info products is open right now and closing fast. The core argument: 100 million people have ChatGPT accounts, roughly 2 million use it effectively, and that 98M-person gap represents people willing to pay for instruction. The author draws an explicit parallel to early adopters of internet skills (1998), social media marketing (2013), and e-commerce — all cases where first movers built significant advantages not from superior skill but from being early and willing to teach.

The article presents five niches (AI for content creators, agencies, specific professions, productivity/automation, entrepreneurs) each framed as a $100k–$1M opportunity, along with a 12-week execution plan from learning to $50k/month. The piece concludes with a pitch for a paid "AI INFO HUB" product. Worth noting: the article conflates genuine skill arbitrage with hype-driven urgency, and the cited revenue figures are unverified.

## Key Takeaways

- The early-adoption arbitrage argument: you need only be 6 months ahead of your target customer, not a domain expert
- AI tools are currently in the productive difficulty zone — complex enough that guidance has value, not yet self-explanatory
- The 18-month closing window claim is the core urgency lever; validity depends on whether AI tool UX actually becomes dramatically simpler
- The playbook (learn → get results → package → sell) is structurally sound as a bootstrap model even if the hype framing is overcooked
- The piece is ultimately a top-of-funnel lead generation post for a paid cohort product
