Seven years of Google Ads experience distilled into 48 operational rules, from someone who lost money early and went on to help brands generate $25M+. The central thesis: Google and Meta are fundamentally different buying environments — Google captures people who are actively searching for solutions, while Meta interrupts passive scrollers. Google's average conversion rate (3–7%) beats Meta's (2–3%) because search intent is warmer.

The article organizes the 48 laws into sections covering ecosystem architecture, campaign structure, targeting, creative, and scaling. The key insight is that successful brands don't run isolated search campaigns — they run interconnected ecosystems where search captures intent, YouTube builds trust, display creates omnipresence, Gmail nurtures consideration, and shopping/brand campaigns close the sale. Each touchpoint is designed to catch the buyer at a different stage of a 7-15 touch journey.

Highlights include treating Google as a multi-channel ecosystem rather than a single ad platform, the importance of match type discipline (starting broad only when conversion data is established), and the discipline of letting data drive creative decisions rather than subjective preference. The author also emphasizes bid strategy maturity — new campaigns should prioritize clicks/conversions before optimizing for target ROAS.

## Key Takeaways

- Google ads convert at 3–7% vs Meta's 2–3% because search intent signals active buying readiness
- Build an interconnected ecosystem across search, YouTube, display, Gmail, and shopping rather than isolated campaigns
- Match types should start restrictive (exact/phrase) and expand only after establishing conversion data
- Creative decisions should be data-driven: test systematically, pause losers fast, scale winners
- New campaigns: maximize clicks first, then switch to target CPA/ROAS once 30–50 conversions are logged
- Shopping feeds require near-perfect data hygiene — poor titles and missing attributes kill visibility
- Brand campaigns protect margins against competitor conquest and should always run
- The full ecosystem approach is what separates $2M+/month brands from those stuck at breakeven
