Dan Koe's comprehensive protocol for identity-level change, arguing that most self-improvement fails because people try to change behavior without changing who they are. The core insight: your current behavior perfectly reflects your current goals — including unconscious ones. Procrastination is a goal (protecting yourself from judgment). Staying in a dead-end job is a goal (safety and predictability). Real change requires changing your point of view, not just your actions.

The article walks through a three-part one-day protocol. Morning: psychological excavation using anti-vision questions ("If nothing changes in 5 years, describe an average Tuesday") to generate productive dissonance, followed by minimum viable vision creation. Midday: scheduled phone reminders to interrupt autopilot thinking at random intervals ("What am I avoiding right now?"). Evening: synthesizing insights into anti-vision/vision statements and a three-horizon goal structure (1-year lens, 1-month project, daily levers). The framework is grounded in ego development theory (9 stages from Loevinger/Cook-Greuter), cybernetics, and Csikszentmihalyi's flow theory — treating your life like a video game with a win condition, stakes, a mission, boss fights, and daily quests.

## Key Takeaways

- All behavior is goal-oriented, including self-sabotaging behavior — the goal just may be unconscious (protection, identity maintenance)
- Real change requires changing identity, not just habits — the successful person doesn't "discipline" themselves to eat healthy, it's just who they are
- Anti-vision is as powerful as vision: generate disgust for the trajectory you're on before orienting toward what you want
- The 9-stage ego development model explains why people get stuck — most hover between stages 4-8 (Self-Aware to Construct-Aware)
- Cybernetics / "the art of getting what you want": intelligence is the ability to sense your position, compare to goal, and iterate
- The one-day protocol: morning excavation → daytime autopilot interrupts → evening synthesis into vision + goals
- Frame life as a video game — anti-vision is what's at stake, 1-year goal is the mission, monthly project is the boss fight, daily levers are quests
