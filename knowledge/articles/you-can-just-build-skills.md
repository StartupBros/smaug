A practical piece on building reusable AI "skills" — structured prompt systems that encode decision frameworks, voice, and complex reasoning — rather than writing prompts from scratch each time. The author describes moving from ad-hoc prompting to systematic skill libraries that run entire workflows autonomously. The core message: you don't need permission or special tools to start building persistent, reusable AI capabilities right now.

The article's full content was not retrieved beyond the preview, but the framing is about the compounding returns of investing in skill-building over one-off prompting — the transition from using AI as a chat interface to treating it as a programmable system of specialists.

## Key Takeaways

- Reusable skills (structured prompt systems) compound in value; one-off prompts do not
- Decision frameworks, tone/voice, and multi-step reasoning can all be encoded as persistent skills
- The barrier to building this kind of system is lower than most people assume — "you can just build skills"
