Christopher Buck, a cancer researcher, engineered a homebrewed beer that functions as a vaccine — using the brewing process as a delivery vector for immunogenic compounds. He is publishing the full process openly so others can replicate it. The piece is framed as a model for developing future edible vaccines, where food and drink serve as accessible drug delivery systems.

## Key Takeaways
- Beer brewing can serve as a practical substrate for vaccine delivery
- The process is being open-sourced so DIY biohackers and researchers can replicate it
- Represents a model for "edible vaccine" development outside traditional pharma pipelines
- Sits alongside the broader trend of personalized/DIY biotech (dog mRNA vaccines, garage gene therapy) gaining mainstream attention
