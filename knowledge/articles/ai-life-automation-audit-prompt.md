A shareable mega-prompt for running a structured AI life automation audit using Claude. The prompt guides an LLM through 9 life domains (career, side hustles, finance, health, productivity, relationships, home, learning, information consumption) in three phases: a conversational questionnaire, an automation map with impact scoring, and a step-by-step implementation guide. The author recommends Claude Opus/Sonnet specifically because the prompt is long and highly structured.

The prompt is delivered in two parts for paste-ability. Phase 1 establishes the AI as a "senior automation strategist" working through domains sequentially, asking no more than 3–5 questions per message. Phase 2 produces a per-domain table of automation opportunities scored on time saved, implementation difficulty, cost tier, and impact level, with a Top 10 Quick Wins summary. Phase 3 generates implementation guides with named tools, numbered steps, pro tips, setup time estimates, and maintenance requirements — including a phased rollout order. The framing is self-promotional but the underlying prompt structure is genuinely comprehensive for anyone who wants to systematically survey their workflow for AI leverage points.

## Key Takeaways

- Nine-domain framework covers the full surface area of a knowledge worker's life (work, side hustle, finance, health, productivity, comms, home, learning, info management)
- Impact scoring on four dimensions — time saved, difficulty, cost, impact level — forces honest triage rather than a grab-bag of suggestions
- Funneling all three phases through a single conversational session with an LLM makes output coherent across domains
- Cap gates principle applies here too: recommending only verifiably real tools, not invented ones
- Particularly useful as a starting template if you want to build a custom agent that periodically audits and suggests workflow improvements
