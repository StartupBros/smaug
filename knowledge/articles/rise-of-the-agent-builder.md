Zach Lloyd (founder of Warp) argues that companies need to hire dedicated "Agent Builders" — full-time roles responsible for replacing SaaS tools and manual workflows with custom AI agents. At his company, the first Agent Builders have already automated fraud detection, competitive intelligence monitoring, and enterprise pilot summaries, cancelling SaaS subscriptions in the process.

The key insight: a great coding agent is also a great general-purpose agent. The new internal mandate shifts from "start with a prompt" to "see if the task can be automated on an ongoing basis."

## Key Takeaways

- Companies should make agent-building a dedicated full-time role, not a side task
- Real automations running: fraud detection (saving tens of thousands of dollars/day), competitive intelligence (replacing half a day of product team work per week), enterprise POC summaries
- Agents are replacing SaaS tools that used to be purchased for social listening, user feedback analysis, etc.
- The main challenges are environment setup, data access grants, monitoring, and prompt tuning via dogfooding
- Unlike Zapier-style "if this, then that" workflows, agents decide their own workflow using intelligence — no predefined steps needed
- Future direction: agents that look at their own past runs and improve themselves autonomously
- Prediction: within a year, every company over 50 people will have at least one Agent Builder
