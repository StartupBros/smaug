A detailed playbook for running 12 simultaneous AI-assisted info product businesses using Claude, arguing that the parallel-launch model beats serial launches on both speed and risk. The author frames the core thesis as "portfolio arbitrage": launching 12 products across 3-4 overlapping niches simultaneously means 8-10 will succeed and only 2-4 will fail, while serial launch takes 18+ months just to reach a first success. Claude handles 80-90% of content creation, compressing 40-hour manual production to roughly 8 hours, making the parallelism feasible for solo operators.

The 90-day per-venture system is highly prescriptive: Days 1-7 use Claude prompts for rapid niche validation (market sizing, competitive gaps, pain points), offer positioning (5 positioning angles per product), and full course outlines with implementation gates. Each venture follows identical launch mechanics — content production, delivery platform setup, traffic systems, and monetization — recycling expertise across the portfolio rather than starting from scratch.

## Key Takeaways

- Portfolio diversification beats serial launches: 12 simultaneous 90-day sprints vs. one 18-month bet
- Claude reduces per-product build time from ~40 hours to ~8 hours, enabling solo operation at scale
- Cluster ventures into 3-4 core categories (e.g., business growth, creator economy, automation) with 2-4 sub-niches each to share underlying research and positioning work
- Specific Claude prompts provided for market validation, positioning statement generation, and course outline creation
- The math: 8-10 winners at $8k-$30k/month each targets $150k-$400k/month portfolio by month 12
