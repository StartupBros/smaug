A founder's practical account of replacing five core SaaS tools with custom-built Lovable apps over a weekend to eliminate per-seat scaling costs ahead of growing to 150 employees. The five replaced tools were a scheduler (booking links), project board (work management), form builder, ticketing platform (event registration), and client portal. Total build time: approximately 15 hours. Reported cost reduction: 98%.

The technical stack is Lovable for the UI layer paired with Supabase for auth and database, which provides the production-grade backend infrastructure that elevates the result from prototype to enterprise-ready. The author's core thesis is that the "build vs. buy" calculation has permanently shifted for non-core CRUD applications — if it's not your company's core IP and it's not fundamentally complex, building in a weekend now beats paying per-seat licenses that scale with headcount. The article serves as a step-by-step replication guide with specific Lovable prompt patterns and Supabase connection instructions for each of the five tools.

## Key Takeaways

- Five tools replaced in ~15 hours: scheduler, project board, form builder, ticketing platform, client portal
- 98% cost reduction vs. SaaS subscriptions that multiply by headcount
- Lovable + Supabase is the production stack: Lovable handles UI/frontend, Supabase provides auth and database instantly
- Build-vs-buy math has flipped for non-core CRUD apps — per-seat pricing makes ownership the rational choice at any meaningful team size
- Zero per-seat scaling costs is the compounding benefit as the company grows
