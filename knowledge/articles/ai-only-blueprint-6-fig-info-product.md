A step-by-step blueprint for running an information product business using exclusively AI tooling, with zero manual work as the core constraint. The central argument is that hybrid workflows (AI for 20% of tasks, manual for 80%) create the creator as the bottleneck, limiting annual revenue to ~$30-50k and requiring 25-30 hours/week. Fully AI-automated operators launch products every 18-21 days, hit $100k/year in 4-5 months, and work ~6 hours weekly. The forcing function — "if a task can't be AI-automated, don't do it" — is what drives systematization.

The recommended tool stack is under $200/month: Manus for market validation (analyzing 5,000+ conversations in 90 min), Claude Pro for curriculum synthesis (200k context for deep research), Grok for conversion copywriting (trained on X viral psychology), Canva for visual assets, ElevenLabs for faceless audio, Make.com for automation, ConvertKit for email, and Gumroad/Teachable for course hosting. The 120-180 day roadmap runs: Days 1-7 validate a problem with Manus, Days 8-21 build curriculum with Claude, Days 22-35 create sales assets with Grok, Days 36-50 publish and launch, then iterate by repeating the cycle for a second product while the first runs.

The key insight is that speed-to-market compounds: launching four AI-only products per year at $2k/month each beats one manually-polished product at $5k/month.

## Key Takeaways

- Full AI-only constraint eliminates you as the bottleneck — hybrid is slow because you're still the bottleneck
- Tool stack under $200/month can support a $100k+/year operation with 99% profit margin on tools
- Manus AI for market research replaces 20 hours of manual Reddit/Twitter browsing with a 90-minute data-driven output
- Grok specifically recommended for sales copy because its training on X data gives it conversion psychology advantages
- Launch cadence matters more than polish: 12-18 days to first revenue beats 8-12 weeks of grinding
- Customer support can be reduced to 15 min/day of AI-draft review vs 2 hours of manual responses
