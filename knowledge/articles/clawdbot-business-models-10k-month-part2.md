Part 2 of a series on monetizing Claude/AI assistant deployments. The article catalogs five service business models built around Clawdbot (Claude-based AI assistants) that the author claims are generating real revenue in early 2026.

The five models are: (1) white-label AI for agencies — build and maintain AI assistants that agencies resell to their clients, charging $2K–5K setup plus $300–800/month per assistant; (2) AI-powered content studio — run a lean production company where AI handles drafts, research, and distribution and humans do quality control, offering blog packages at $2K–5K/month and social media management at $3K–8K/month; (3) niche vertical solutions — build complete AI systems for a single industry (real estate, e-commerce, coaching) sold as turnkey packages at $3K–10K setup plus $500–1.5K/month recurring; (4) AI implementation consulting — audit business workflows, identify high-ROI automation opportunities, and sell a prioritized roadmap with optional done-for-you build; (5) paid community for AI operators — build a membership community with templates, live calls, and peer support, targeting 200–500 members at $29–99/month.

The recurring theme is that margins are high because AI handles production-level work that previously required multiple human staff.

## Key Takeaways

- White-label AI for agencies creates recurring revenue without direct client contact — you maintain the assistants, agencies own the relationships
- Content studio model inverts the staffing assumption: AI does 80% of production, humans do QC — a 3-person shop can output what previously required 10–15 people
- Niche vertical specificity is a pricing lever — "AI for Amazon wholesale sellers" justifies higher fees than generic "AI for e-commerce"
- Implementation consulting (audit + roadmap) doubles as a sales funnel for done-for-you builds
- Paid community monetization scales via member count: 200 members at $49/month = ~$10K MRR without any per-client delivery work
