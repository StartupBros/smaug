Garry Tan's essay argues that AI has removed every technical barrier that previously served as cover for not building. The old excuses — "I can't edit," "I don't know how to code," "I'm not technical" — no longer hold. Intelligence has become too cheap to meter. What remains as the differentiator is taste (your ability to evaluate your own work and know when it falls short) and agency (your desire to actually win and keep going). The moment you can perceive the gap between your output and what's good is exactly the moment most people quit — AI just makes it harder to hide at that gap.

## Key Takeaways

- AI eliminates technical skill barriers; taste and agency are now the real competitive advantages
- The ability to recognize that your work isn't good yet is a feature, not a reason to stop
- "Intelligence is officially too cheap to meter" — framing intelligence as a commodity changes what bottlenecks matter
- Directed at founders and builders: the cost of starting has dropped to near zero, so inaction is a choice
