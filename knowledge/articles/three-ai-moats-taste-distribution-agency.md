A long-form argument that as AI commoditizes the ability to produce output, three human qualities become the only durable moats: taste, distribution, and high agency. The window to build these advantages is roughly 12 months before the gap between lazy and intentional AI usage shrinks.

## Key Takeaways
- 90% of AI users follow the same loop: prompt → accept → ship. This makes even one moat immediately differentiating.
- **Taste** is not abstract — it's knowing when to override the AI's defaults. LLMs are probability machines that statistically default to the most common patterns (shadcn/ui, Inter font, purple gradients). Taste is having explicit "what to avoid" rules.
- The 80/20 rule: let AI handle 80% (research, first drafts, boilerplate, structure). Pour your judgment into the last 20% — hooks, curation, decisions.
- **Distribution** runs on a different clock than technology. AI can compress creation from days to minutes; trust still takes months or years. Passive audiences are commodities; active communities are moats.
- **High agency** is the willingness to figure things out without a tutorial. It's a multiplier: curiosity × AI = 10x leverage; passivity × AI = 0.
- AI is not an equalizer — it's a multiplier that amplifies whatever the user brings to it. Bad judgment + AI just gets you to the wrong place faster.
- "Good enough" is now free and infinite. Remarkable still costs something, measured in taste, attention, and the willingness to go beyond the first output.
