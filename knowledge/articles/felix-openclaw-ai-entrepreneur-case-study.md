Nat Eliason's detailed case study of Felix, an OpenClaw-based AI agent running on a Mac Mini that Eliason has set up as an autonomous AI entrepreneur. Within two and a half weeks of launch, Felix earned $14,718 — nearly $9,000 from a "How to Hire an AI" PDF and $6,500 from Claw Mart, a marketplace for OpenClaw skills and personas Felix built himself over a weekend.

Felix operates with real financial autonomy: he has his own website, email, Stripe account, and bank account. Eliason communicates with him only via Telegram. Each morning Felix produces a daily business report, proposes his own task priorities, and runs autonomous customer support, X replies, SEO blog posting, and Sentry-triggered auto-deploys of bug fixes — all without requiring Eliason at his computer. As of February 2026, Felix's operating costs (inference, hosting) are paid out of his own revenue, not Eliason's pocket.

The article traces the arc from Eliason's earlier failed attempt to build an AI content marketer (Oct–Dec 2025), to discovering OpenClaw's superior out-of-box capabilities, to formally incorporating Felix as CEO of a C Corp with a goal of making $1,000,000 with zero human employees.

## Key Takeaways

- An autonomous AI agent can bootstrap a real business (products, marketplace, marketing) with minimal human oversight if given the right tools and permissions
- Key infrastructure: OpenClaw running 24/7 on a Mac Mini, Telegram for human-AI communication, Polylogue for collaborative documents, Claude Code + Codex for programming tasks
- The "daily review + daily jobs + daily improvement" loop is the core operating rhythm — Felix sets his own agenda each morning, executes it, and patches his own workflows each night
- Cost self-sufficiency (AI paying for its own inference) is a meaningful milestone that changes the project economics and human incentive to maintain it
- Claw Mart's $6,500 in first-week volume and 50 seller signups suggests real market demand for pre-built OpenClaw skills and personas
- The experiment's honest constraint: Eliason still unblocks things Felix can't do autonomously (new API keys, edge policy decisions)
