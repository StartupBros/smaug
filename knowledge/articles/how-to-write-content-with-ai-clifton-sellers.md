Clifton Sellers, who claims $4M in agency revenue built on founder-led content, presents a contrarian take on AI writing: use it to think more clearly and pressure-test ideas, not to generate output. The premise is that AI produces polished nonsense when fed vague thinking but becomes a powerful amplifier when given conviction and lived experience. The article delivers seven concrete prompts, each with a specific adversarial or analytical role.

The prompts are framed as systems: a Belief Stress Test that forces explicit articulation of core convictions, an Experience Miner that extracts non-replicable insights from decisions and mistakes, a Clarity Destroyer that eliminates vague language under scrutiny, a Content-as-Infrastructure prompt that maps how one idea creates compounding assets, a Voice Integrity Filter that guards against artificial polish, a Silent Sales Map that evaluates whether content reduces buying friction, and a Weekly Operator prompt that enforces focus under constraint. The throughline is that the person must already have opinions worth sharpening — AI cannot supply those.

## Key Takeaways

- Core thesis: AI is a pressure-tester and amplifier, not a generator — vague inputs produce polished garbage
- Seven adversarial prompt roles: belief stress-tester, experience miner, clarity destroyer, content infrastructure designer, voice integrity filter, sales friction analyzer, weekly focus operator
- The prerequisite is "scar tissue" — lived decisions, mistakes, and non-negotiable positions that can't be fabricated
- Content-as-infrastructure framing: optimize for longevity and authority compounding over short-term reach or novelty
- Voice integrity prompt specifically prohibits motivational framing, filler transitions, and sentences written to impress rather than explain
