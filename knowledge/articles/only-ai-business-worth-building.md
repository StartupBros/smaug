A long-form business model analysis from a founder who spent $150K testing AI business models before concluding that software (SaaS) dominates on every dimension worth tracking: time freedom, recurring revenue, exit potential, and future-proofing against AI displacement. The article scores popular models (AI agencies, faceless channels, freelance dev, dropshipping, day trading) on four criteria and finds them all lacking. Software wins because it scales without the creator, generates MRR/ARR, commands high acquisition multiples, and gets better automatically as underlying AI models improve. Supported with five case studies: Base44 ($80M acquisition), N8N ($2.5B valuation), Lovable ($100M ARR in 8 months), Levels.io ($3.5M/year solo), and Vectal ($155K ARR). The article ends with a sales pitch for the author's accelerator program, but the analytical framework is substantive.

## Key Takeaways

- AI automation agencies score poorly on all four criteria; every agency owner the author knows wants to quit
- Freelance AI dev is being undercut by Cursor and Claude Code — contract volume has already declined since ChatGPT launched
- Day trading with AI bots is the worst model: 97% of traders who persist 300+ days lose money per peer-reviewed data
- Software wins because it auto-evolves as AI improves, can hit 70-98% margins, and has real exit value
- You don't need a following or investors; N8N, Base44, and Midjourney all grew without either
- Delay raising VC funding as long as possible; nearly every successful founder gives the same advice
- The key skill for the AI era: building, improving, and overseeing workflows that AI agents execute
