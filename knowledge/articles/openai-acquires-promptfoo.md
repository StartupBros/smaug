OpenAI announced the acquisition of Promptfoo, an open-source AI security testing platform used by 25%+ of the Fortune 500 to identify and remediate vulnerabilities in AI systems during development. Promptfoo had raised $23M at an $86M valuation, backed by a16z and Insight Partners, with 300,000+ developers using its CLI. The technology will strengthen agentic security testing and evaluation capabilities in OpenAI Frontier, and Promptfoo will remain open source under its current license.

The real acquisition thesis, per analyst commentary: OpenAI bought 125+ pre-approved Fortune 500 enterprise relationships and a procurement shortcut through enterprise CISOs — distribution that would have taken Frontier years to build organically.

## Key Takeaways
- Promptfoo was used by over 125 Fortune 500 companies in production as a CI/CD pipeline for AI red-teaming
- Acquired for its enterprise distribution and pre-approved vendor trust, not just its technology
- Remains open source; current customers continue to be supported
- Illustrates that in the enterprise agent platform war, the bottleneck is procurement approval, not model intelligence
