A 12-point strategic playbook for building durable leverage with AI rather than chasing hype. The central argument is to reframe the question from "how do I make money with AI" to "where can AI give me compounding leverage" — treating AI as a multiplier across code, content, systems, and judgment rather than a standalone tool. The piece argues for vertical specialization over horizontal plays, selling outcomes (saved hours, reduced errors, conversion rates) rather than technology stacks, and building proof before building software by doing manual work for a few clients first, then productizing the repeatable patterns.

## Key Takeaways

- Stack five leverage types: code, capital, content, systems, and judgment — AI amplifies all of them
- Start from existing strengths ("what am I already good at that AI can 10x?") rather than asking what AI business to start
- Target "painkiller" problems: manual, repetitive, time-consuming, hated, or expensive-when-wrong
- Go vertical and narrow — horizontal AI builders compete with everyone, vertical builders compete with almost nobody
- Sell ROI outcomes (hours saved, error rates, conversion lift), not embeddings or model versions
- Path to productization: do it manually for 3 clients → notice repeats → turn repeat into a tool → charge monthly
- Integration fluency (connecting CRM, email, calendar, docs, Slack, analytics) enables end-to-end automation that changes businesses
- Public documentation of your work builds audience, proof of credibility, and feedback loops simultaneously
