The author argues that AI has fundamentally broken the economics of the traditional agency model. YC's thesis — "don't sell the software to the client; use it yourself and sell finished work at 100x the price" — means the build-vs-buy equation has flipped. An agency is now an incubator for its own SaaS products.

The author draws on his experience co-founding Divante (built Vue Storefront and Open Loyalty as internal IP that became global products) and Callstack to outline a "Zone to Win" framework: agencies must simultaneously operate a stable revenue core, optimize operations, incubate experimental products, and at some point make a full-company bet on one incubation project as their next core business. The article warns against common traps: using benched talent for product work (requires a dedicated team and committed leadership duo with equity), and "carving out" client code as a product (fails because client-specific builds need to be rebuilt from scratch as standalone entities).

## Key Takeaways

- Agencies that use AI to deliver "finished goods" instead of billing hours can command software-like margins and much higher valuations (e.g., $5M vs $25M for same revenue/profit)
- "Zone to Win" strategy: run four parallel zones (performance, productivity, incubation, transformation) — most agencies only operate zones 1 and 3 and never commit to a successor business
- Never build a product with "free" bench talent; it requires a dedicated team (100% focus) with a two-pizza leadership duo (Tech + Business) and meaningful equity
- Don't carve out client code — the know-how transfers but the product must be rebuilt from scratch as a standalone entity
- Self-funding a product to sustainability typically requires $1M–$3M investment before spinning out
- Author's current project, Open Mercato, applies these principles as open-source ERP/CRM with AI-assisted coding built in
