A 15-year YouTube operator's counter-intuitive playbook for making real money on the platform. The core thesis: high-production "shiny" niches are negative EV; the real money is in unglamorous, specific niches with low competition. The article argues for faceless channels over personality-driven ones because they scale via workflow rather than creator presence, and they're insulated from personal crises. Supporting data: over 40% of total YouTube watch time now comes from TV, where viewers average 8+ extra minutes per session — meaning TV optimization (longer videos) is the highest-leverage structural bet you can make.

## Key Takeaways

- Niche specificity beats production quality for monetization; obscure topics often outperform expensive documentaries
- TV accounts for 40%+ of total watch time and 42.6% of watch minutes on the author's own channel — optimize video length accordingly
- Faceless channels (like The Infographics Show, 400M+ views/year) compound via workflow; personality brands depend on the creator showing up
- Creative arbitrage is real: an 8-hour loop of mice walking generated approximately $1M in revenue
- Stop consuming YouTube content; only open the app to scout angles and patterns
