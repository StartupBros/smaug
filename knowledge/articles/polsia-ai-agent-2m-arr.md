Andrew Warner's breakdown of Polsia, Ben Cera's AI agent platform that has built 2,849 companies at $2.1M ARR. Cera is a former Cloud Kitchens employee #1 (Travis Kalanick's post-Uber venture).

The business model is nuanced: most of the $2.1M ARR comes from founders paying Polsia $50/month to build and run their businesses — not from revenue generated by those businesses' end customers.

## Key Takeaways

- **Human kickoff required**: A person with domain expertise and taste provides the initial business idea — AI does not yet have this judgment
- **Agent stack**: Claude Opus plans the business, agents build a web MVP (similar to Lovable), cold email agents find and contact prospects using paid databases
- **Marketing beast**: Sora 2 generates fake UGC-style video ads; Meta ad spend is what drove the revenue inflection — not cold email
- **Business model reality**: Revenue is primarily subscription fees from founders, not customer revenue from the 2,849 businesses built
- **Founder payments**: Founders pay for ads, extra coding work, and marketing campaigns on top of the $50/month base fee
