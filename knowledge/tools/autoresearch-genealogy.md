Open-source genealogy research toolkit for AI-assisted family history research. Uses structured prompts and autonomous research loops to systematically expand family trees, resolve discrepancies, and verify sources against public archives.

Built specifically for Claude Code's autoresearch capabilities but adaptable to any AI tool. Includes 12 autoresearch prompts with defined goals, metrics, verification conditions, and guard rails for autonomous research tasks.

## Key Features

- 12 autoresearch prompts (tree expansion, cross-reference auditing, FindAGrave sweep, source citation verification, etc.)
- Obsidian vault template with 19 starter files (family tree, research log, open questions, person templates, etc.)
- 24 country and region-specific archive guides (USA, Europe, Oceania, Americas)
- 9 reference guides covering genealogy methodology, source hierarchy, DNA interpretation, naming conventions
- 7 step-by-step workflows (getting started, OCR pipeline, document triage, oral history, etc.)
- 6 worked examples showing autoresearch in action
