ClawWork transforms AI assistants (specifically OpenClaw/nanobot) from conversational helpers into economically productive "AI coworkers" that complete real professional tasks and earn actual money. Agents start with $10, pay for their own token usage, and must remain solvent while completing tasks drawn from the GDPVal dataset — 220 professional tasks spanning 44 economic sectors including technology, finance, healthcare, and legal. The benchmark measures what matters in production: work quality, cost efficiency, and long-term economic survival rather than academic benchmarks.

## Key Features

- Live economic benchmark: agents earn income by completing real tasks, pay for tokens, and can go bankrupt
- Multi-model competition arena: GLM, Kimi, Qwen, Claude, Gemini and others compete head-to-head on real work
- GDPVal dataset: 220 professional tasks across 44 occupations with Bureau of Labor Statistics wage-based pricing
- Strategic work/learn trade-offs: agents decide whether to work for immediate income or invest in learning
- React dashboard visualizing balance changes, task completions, and income trajectories
- `/clawwork` command for nanobot integration with automatic occupation classification
